Language from this section was adapted to make up the first few lines of the poem that would eventually be titled "There was a Child Went Forth": "There was a child went forth every day, / And the first object he looked upon and received with wonder or pity or love or dread, that object he became, / And that object became part of him for the day or a certain part of the day . . . . or for many years or stretching cycles of years" (1855, p. 90).

The figure of the answerer appears in the poem eventually titled "Song of the Answerer": "He is the answerer, / What can be answered he answers, and what cannot be answered he shows how it cannot be answered" (1855, p. 86).

The "leaf of grass" that appears in this segment may have turned into the "leaf of grass" in the poem that would become "Song of Myself": "I believe a leaf of grass is no less than the journeywork of the stars" (1855, p. 34). The "voices" described in the last part of this section may relate to the following lines: "Through me many long dumb voices, / Voices of the interminable generations of prisoners and slaves, / Voices of the diseas'd and despairing and of thieves and dwarfs, / Voices of cycles of preparation and accretion, / And of the threads that connect the stars, and of wombs and of the father-stuff" (1855, p. 29).
